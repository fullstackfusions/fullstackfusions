📋 Scorecard by Category

Total Net Worth (30yr): Detached wins — $5.33M vs $4.60M. The land appreciation advantage of a detached home outweighs the condo's better investment surplus.
Investment Portfolio: Condo wins — $3.46M vs $2.12M. The extra ~$690/mo surplus (yr 1) compounding at 15% creates a massive portfolio. But note: condo goes cash-flow negative ~yr 14 as fees escalate.
Property Asset Value: Detached wins decisively — $3.21M vs $1.14M. You own land. Land in GTA is finite. This is the single biggest wealth driver.
Family & Kids: Detached wins — Space, backyard, basement, 4 parking. Non-negotiable advantages for raising kids.
Guests & Hosting: Detached wins — Guest suite in basement, 4 parking spots, large entertaining space.
Monthly Cash Flow: Condo wins early — More investable cash in years 1–13. But the maintenance fee becomes a serious liability by year 14+.
Lifestyle Convenience: Condo wins — Gym, pool included, less maintenance responsibility, newer building. Great for young professionals, less suited for families.
Long-term Asset Quality: Detached wins — Generational asset, land ownership, rental income, solar potential, no board risk, full renovation freedom.
Risk: Special Assessments — Condo owners face unpredictable special assessments ($5–30K). Detached maintenance is predictable and your choice.

🎯 Mentor's Recommendation

For your life stage — go Detached. You have a wife, you're building toward leadership, you value privacy and control. The basement rental offsets cost significantly. The land is yours. The numbers say detached wins by $736K at 30 years. But more importantly — this is a home, not just an investment. Backyard, guest suite, full autonomy, and no condo board telling you what to do with your property.
The immediate $15K fix cost is a one-time hurdle, not a dealbreaker. Electrical panel to 200A is actually an upgrade, not just a repair — it unlocks EV charging, solar, and higher resale value. Treat it as value-add capital, not sunk cost.
Solar panels are a compounding return asset. North-south facing roof = ideal solar exposure in GTA. Install in year 2–3 after upfront fixes. Ontario net metering + $15–25K install could generate $1,500–2,500/yr in savings + sell-back. That's 6–10% annual return on install cost — better than most bonds.
Budget note: Your $363/mo investable amount is tight in Year 1, but the rental income ($1,000/mo) is your real wealth buffer. Optimize the basement unit, sign a 1-year lease, and treat that as locked income. By Year 10, rental grows to ~$1,344/mo and your investable amount slowly increases.
Think like a Principal Engineer: The detached home is a platform, not just a dwelling — it gives you optionality. You can add an ADU (accessory dwelling unit) under Ontario's "More Homes Built Faster Act", potentially adding $800–1,200/mo income. You can sever the lot in 15–20 years if the city allows. You can refinance to fund your FullStackFusions or Localz ventures. A condo traps you. Land frees you.
